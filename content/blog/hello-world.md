For a long time, whenever I had to choose between **poking at something new and interesting** and **turning a solved problem into a clear write-up**, I almost always picked the first option. At best I’d push a rough experiment to GitHub and move on. Polishing the idea into a post rarely felt worth the time.

That was a reasonable trade-off: exploration is fun, and “documentation energy” is finite.

Things changed with **AI assistants**. Drafting structure, tightening wording, and catching blind spots in an explanation is much cheaper than it used to be. The bottleneck is no longer “can I afford to write this?” but “is the problem actually worth sharing?”

So this blog is a small shift in habit: **not throwing away** neat solutions to real problems—**packaging them** as short, careful posts instead of leaving them only in a branch or a throwaway repo.

If you’re reading this: welcome. I’m still learning how to balance depth and brevity; the goal is posts that would have helped me a week before I solved each thing.

*Yet another attempt for the reader to become a writer—this time with better odds.*
